Rolf Fyne

Managing Director, Founder

Rolf Fyne has wide ranging industrial and technology experience that has focused on delivery of proprietary business development, threat management and new business programs for European and US multi-nationals. He holds a Honours Bachelor Degree in Mechanical Engineering from Sheffield University in the UK.

Rolf’s early career started in the aerospace industry in the UK. He later joined the Anglo-American Corporation, Johannesburg, working on large scale mining and metallurgical plants in Central and Southern Africa in plant and divisional engineering roles and then as project manager. Soon after completing his MBA at the Manchester Business School, he returned to the UK to become the Managing Director of an international industrial ventilation subsidiary and, subsequently, CEO of a construction tools company. Following this, he formed his own international business development consultancy with a client list including ABB, DuPont, Bayer, BASF, GE, DSM, Dow, Eaton and AlliedSignal

He has a comprehensive understanding of European and North American international business, industry, finance, sales and in-bound investment in both directions.  From early 2003, he fulfilled the role of International Business Development Consultant Europe for the Ontario Ministry of Economic Development and Trade for the Environmental and Renewable Technologies and Advanced Manufacturing sectors. Recently, he has also been acting as an "enabler" for hi-tech related start-ups providing market and business development inputs.  Rolf was appointed in October 2005 as European In-Market Representative for British Columbia for inward investment and trade for the advanced manufacturing, environmental, renewable and power technologies sectors.

Rainer M. Giersch

Managing Director, Founder

Mr. Rainer Giersch completed his studies in economics with a Ph. D in Linear Economics. Upon completion of his PhD thesis, Mr. Giersch joined British Petroleum in Germany as press officer and was soon promoted to deputy chief press officer.   Shortly afterwards he was called to the British Petroleum board's staff with responsibilities for the special projects group. Rainer continued at British Petroleum in roles including strategic business development with BP Exploration in London and unit-manager strategic corporate planning.

Rainer Giersch left BP in 1993 to become Managing Partner of RCN Marketing GmbH, an international strategic marketing and marketing development agency. In this role, Mr. Giersch provided advice to many blue chip companies, governments institutions and associations on industry sector focused strategies, mergers & acquisitions preparations, and activities in attracting foreign direct investment in various countries.

Mr. Giersch has also chaired the British Chamber of Commerce in Germany's Steering Committee and is now member of the British Chamber's board and council as well as the chairman of the Chamber's Regional Group Northern Germany. In addition to this, he is also chairman of the supervisory board of Institut für Telekommunikation Aktiengesellschaft, active in the communications technology and services sector and still maintains his university links as lecturer at Hamburg University.

He is member of the committee of the German-Canadian Business-Club Hamburg and a member of the German-Canadian Association. He now operates his own firm: International Business Development - Dr. R.M. Giersch. Seven years ago Rainer was appointed as Director Business Development Europe for the Ministry of Technology and Economic Development British Columbia for inward investment and. He is one of the founders of Accordo Partners.
